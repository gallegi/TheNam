# Hello World

Welcome to my blog. If you are reading this, you have found the very first post on what I hope will become a useful corner of the internet for ML practitioners and curious engineers.

I have been working in machine learning for several years now, and one pattern I keep noticing is how much institutional knowledge stays locked inside company Slack channels, private notebooks, and the heads of engineers who simply never had time to write things down. This blog is my small attempt to push back against that tendency.

Writing has a way of forcing clarity. When I explain something in prose, I discover the gaps in my own understanding. Over the years, some of my best debugging breakthroughs have come not from staring at code, but from writing down what I thought the code was doing — and noticing the contradiction. I want to bring that same discipline to sharing ideas publicly.

What will I write about here? Mostly machine learning engineering — the practical side of building systems that actually work in production. That means MLOps, experiment tracking, data pipelines, model serving, and the occasionally painful lessons that come from deploying models to real users. I will also write about software engineering principles that apply to ML, and occasionally about the craft of technical writing itself. I hope you will find something useful here.
